Hi! I’m Arielle, a Brooklyn-based interdisciplinary designer, developer and artist. I am currently pursuing a graduate degree at ITP, where I explore how art and technology can improve how we understand and share stories about our increasingly complex reality.

Through identifying and interpreting existing systems – ecological, social, cultural – my work aims to cultivate more meaningful, sustainable, and connective experiences. I enjoy working across multiple disciplines, including web and mobile, graphics and illustration, live video, installations, performance, and connected hardware.

THE INTERNET OF LIVING THINGS

My ITP thesis project, an exploration of connected objects that extends the Internet of Things to include living objects including plants, animals and microbes.

MYCELIAL FORMS

Check out documentation of my progress on this project on my ITP blog.

Also visit my tumblr blog where I am keeping a record of all my mycelium inspirations and experiments.

MIRROR SNAPS

A hidden-camera mirror photobooth project that generates animated gifs and immediately posts them to a website for people to download and share. Built in Javascript using websockets and node.js.

Visit the dedicated project website at mirrorsnaps.me

CIRCUIT DESIGN

A few examples of my work creating custom circuit boards. All board designs were created in Eagle CAD. I have experience with both etching and routing production techniques.

Check out my ITP blog for more documentation of the process of designing and producing these boards.

MICROBIOME PORTRAITURE

A public art intervention project and exploration of the relationship between microbiomes and the self.
